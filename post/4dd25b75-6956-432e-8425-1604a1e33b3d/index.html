The benefit in 'Archaic' Email interfaces

Aug 10, 2017 2 min read

I started out as a organised person who liked neat handwriting, extensive notes and a systematic approach to doing things.

It’s been a couple of years that I’ve been using mu4e as my primary driver for dealing with email. Though I do on occasion rely on the web based interface, using mu4e has enabled me to master my email, like no other tool that I’ve ever used. Before using mu4e, and the fastmail setup, I never really had any grip of my email inbox and could never leverage and utilise the wealth of data that lurks in each corner.

There’s a wide variety of ways in which people prefer to access their email. There’s a lot of focus on interface design nowadays and the experience on each modality is unique in the possibilities of both focussed and lateral workflows, speed.

Web mail is probably the most predominant way in which people access their emails.

Being an emacs fan, and heavily relying on org-mode for my tasks

I’ve typically been overwhelmed by email. I’ve often had the feeling of not utilising the potential of email, because the volume of unread email can build up so quickly. At the same time, there’s also a lot of interesting information out that could be useful. It’s all well to create clever filters in gmail and have emails plop into the ‘right folder’. However, atleast in my case - a few weeks of intense activity not related to emails, and I forget the so called arrangement I had set up. An arrangement that is easily forgotten is probably not a great arrangement.
